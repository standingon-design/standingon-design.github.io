Project Description

Trimester Four introduced my first commercial project. The client, Merivale Group, wanted to expand their hospitality empire into Melbourne with an original project at 406 Park St, Albert Park. Merivale’s portfolio is diverse covering everything from the neighborhood pub, to Hong Kong inspired dining. A clear European influence can be seen in the groups design language, blended with a clean “Sydney” Aesthetic.


To stay true to the group’s identity the proposed design takes influence from the history of wine making in Europe which originated in Georgia over 4000 years ago. Georgia’s greatest styles are those whites that ferment on their skins, gaining deeper hues of orange and yellow, and colloquially known as Orange wine. Orange wines are generally organic/bio-dynamic and represent a return to artisan processes and the worship of good food and drink. 406 Park Street embraces this return to agriculture as culture and features a ground-floor cafe and bistro, and an upstairs bottleshop featuring a heavy selection of natural wines and local small-goods. Lighter colours and clean lines inform the interior spaces with raw materials such as stone and wood contrasting polished titanium cladding.
